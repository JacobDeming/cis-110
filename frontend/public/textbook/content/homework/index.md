# Overview

There are two types of assignments in this course: **Practice Exams** and **Coding Contributions**.

## Practice Exams:
These homework assignments involve making a video of yourself taking a practice exam. Each week, the number of questions you will need to answer will increase, so you will be gradually working through more content per exam as the course progresses.

In your videos, you should make the an effort to answer all the questions out loud even if you're unsure of the answer. If you don't know the answer to a question, it's okay to say so, but you should still try to relate the question to the relevant part of the "Table of Contents" diagram discussed at the start of the course.

The videos should start recording before you generate the practice exam, so I can see you press the button to create it. During the recording, you must be alone in a room and state clearly that you are not using any notes or textbooks. There should be no editing or cuts—just a continuous recording in which I can (1) hear your voice and (2) can clearly see your face, your screen, and any diagrams you draw.

**The goal of these videos isn't to get everything right but to familiarize yourself with the exam format and requirements. They serve as practice to help you prepare for the actual midterm and final exams.**

## Equipment

To successfully complete these assignments, you will need several pieces of equipment and software tools. At a minimum, you should have:

* A Camera or Webcam: This will be used to record yourself during the video, demonstrating that you are alone in the room and providing a visual of your face and surroundings.

* A Microphone: Essential for capturing clear audio of your voice as you answer questions and explain your reasoning throughout the recording.

* Screen Recording Software: You will need a program that can record your computer screen so that the exam generation and questions are visible. I personally recommend [OBS Studio](https://obsproject.com) since it is free and fairly easy to use.

Diagram Creation Tools: You can use a computer program (such as drawing software, online diagram tools, or presentation apps) to create diagrams. Alternatively, you can record yourself drawing diagrams on paper or a whiteboard with a camera or document camera.
